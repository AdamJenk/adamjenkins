About Me

Welcome! I am Research and Teaching Fellow in Cybersecurity at King's College London. My research focuses on the intersection of security, privacy, AI and human-computer interaction, often known as Usable Security and Privacy (USP) I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Edinburgh in 2023, where I was advised by Dr. Kami Vaniea and Dr. Maria Wolters. Before joining King's College London, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Edinburgh, working with the TULiPS lab on Phishing advice generation with AI. My work has been published in top venues including CHI, CSCW, and IEEE S&P. I am passionate about developing usable security and privacy solutions that protect users while maintaining a positive user experience.

Research Interests

Usable Security & Privacy

Developing user-friendly security mechanisms that people can understand and use effectively in their daily digital interactions.

Human Factors in Cybersecurity

Understanding how human behavior, cognition, and decision-making impact security outcomes in complex systems, particularly Phishing decisions.

Privacy-Preserving Technologies

Creating innovative solutions that protect user privacy while enabling useful functionality in modern applications.

Security Education

Researching effective methods for teaching security concepts and raising awareness about digital threats.

Design Methods for Accessible Privacy and Security

I am interested in exploring the use of co-design and co-creation methods in USP research, and the potential benefits with working with margenalised groups
